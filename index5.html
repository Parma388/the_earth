The Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. About 70% of Earth's surface is covered with water, while the remaining 30% is land, making it appear mostly blue from space. Earth is part of the Milky Way galaxy and orbits the Sun along with seven other major planets. The planet has a protective atmosphere composed mainly of nitrogen and oxygen, which sustains life and shields us from harmful solar radiation.

Earth’s shape is not a perfect sphere; it is slightly flattened at the poles and bulges at the equator. The planet has one natural satellite, the Moon, which influences tides and stabilizes Earth’s rotation. Mountains, plains, valleys, and hills are some of the major landforms present on Earth. Earth is about 4.54 billion years old and its core is as hot as the surface of the Sun. The Earth’s rotation causes day and night, while its revolution around the Sun causes the changing of seasons. Plate tectonics shape Earth's surface, leading to earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and mountain formation.
